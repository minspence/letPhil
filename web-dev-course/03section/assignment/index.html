My first "filming experience"

Okay so I was 16 years old and I was very interested in free running/parkour and martial arts. Well on day I was practicing some flips and various kicks when some guy came up to me and asked if I wanted to be in a video he was making for film school. Now every alarm that should have been sounding to tell me "Hey bad idea, you don't know this person!" was completely ignored by my 16 year old brain.


So I agreed and we met up a few days late to film. We went way up into Pilot Mountain to a random house they were using for the film, again all alarms where completely ignored. This story could have ended very badly. I played the role of an young assassin hunting a reporter and a kid that had witnessed a crime. I was supposed to die in the scene and was also responsible for all the stunts and fight choregraphy.


I was very excited to be in a film and to do all the stunts, but I was also very nervous. The filming went on for a few hours and I had a lot of fun. I was doing all the stunts and fight choreography. But I didn't want to back out and disappoint the guy who had asked me to be in the film. So I just kept going and tried my best. In the end, we finished filming and I was very proud of myself for doing all the stunts and fight choreography without getting hurt. It was a great experience and I'm glad I did it.
